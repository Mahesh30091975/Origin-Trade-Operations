Coffee Trade Operations — Process Flow

Onboarding Playbook for New Joiners
v 1.0 CEO · Gurpreet Vohra
London · Denmark · Kenya
Source of truth: Phlo ERP
Welcome to Trade Ops. This flow walks you from buyer onboarding through to cash collection — 22 steps across 4 phases. Every step lives in Phlo, every dollar of price risk is managed via hedging, and every contract is monitored against a live credit limit. Read the green “Trade Ops Nuance” box on each step — that is the institutional knowledge the document alone won’t teach you.
01

Onboarding — Setting the Foundation

Before any deal moves
1

KYC & Credit Verification

Verify identity, trade references, and credit limits for both supplier and customer before any deal is signed. The buyer’s profile is submitted to credit agencies who assign exposure limits underwritten against buyer default. Monitor live exposure per buyer — do not sign new contracts until an existing limit is restored.

Trade Ops Nuance Credit insurers (Atradius, Coface, Allianz Trade) underwrite the buyer — not the trade. If their limit drops, your unshipped sales contract becomes unhedged credit risk. Reconcile insurer limits weekly against open AR + open sales.
2

Business Confirmation

Email Gurpreet to formally confirm deal terms. This is the trigger for all downstream activity. Always include the current credit exposure against that buyer so Gurpreet has full visibility before approving.

Trade Ops Nuance Standard template: origin, grade, qty (bags & MT), price basis (fixed vs differential to ICE), shipment window, payment terms, Incoterm, and existing exposure. Without this header data, the contract cannot be parameterised in Phlo.
3

Cargo Insurance — Monthly Declaration

On the 1st of every month, file the monthly declaration so all active shipments are covered. The policy runs warehouse-to-warehouse: from the first warehouse at origin, through marine transit, to the first warehouse at discharge port.

Trade Ops Nuance Marine open cover is declaration-based — a missed declaration = an uninsured shipment. If a vessel sinks on the 5th and you declared on the 7th, the underwriter can deny the claim. Diarise this for the 1st, no exceptions.
02

Purchase Track — From Contract to Cargo on the Water

Origin → Vessel → Port
4

Purchase Contract

Prepare the purchase contract on Phlo within 24 hours of business confirmation.

Trade Ops Nuance Use ECF / ECC standard forms where possible. Lock in arbitration jurisdiction (typically London, ECF rules). Differential contracts must state fixation window — the buyer’s window to call price against the underlying ICE month.
5

Purchase Order

Raise the PO on Phlo on the same day as the contract. This links the signed contract to an actual procurement order in the system.

Trade Ops Nuance The PO commits inventory, costs, and FX exposure. If the PO and contract reference numbers don’t match in Phlo, downstream reports (P&L, position, hedge ratio) will all reconcile incorrectly.
6

PSS Workflow — Sample Request

Request a Pre-Shipment Sample from the seller and submit it to QA at least 6 weeks before scheduled shipment.

Trade Ops Nuance 6 weeks is not arbitrary: roast + cup + tasting cycle = ~10 days, plus rejection-and-replacement buffer of 2–3 weeks at origin before the vessel cut-off. Miss the window and you either ship blind or roll the booking — both cost money.
7

PSS Approval by QA

QA reviews and approves the sample within 3 days of receipt. Nothing proceeds without this. Phlo blocks invoice generation and warehouse release until approval is granted.

Trade Ops Nuance QA outcome is binary in Phlo (Approved / Rejected / Conditional). A “Conditional” flag — e.g. moisture out-of-spec but cuppable — should be flagged to trading desk same day; it may change the differential or trigger a discount negotiation with the seller.
8

Shipping Instructions Issued

Issue Shipping Instructions to the seller on the same day PSS approval is received.

Trade Ops Nuance SI must specify: marks & numbers, consignee, notify party, BL release type (original vs telex/express), and HS codes. Errors here become BL amendments later — expensive and slow.
9

Freight Booking (FOB — we pay freight)

Seller advises cargo readiness and available lines. If we hold a contract rate more favourable than spot, we share our contract reference; seller books under it, freight remains payable by us. Seller then collects containers via delivery order, stuffs, and ships.

Containers are not released until (a) the supplier is paid in full and all cargo docs are in our possession, and (b) the shipping line is paid all freight, demurrage, detention and THC.

Selection criteria, in order: lowest freight · shortest transit · sailing frequency. A premium for a shorter voyage or schedule fit is sometimes justified — document the rationale.

Trade Ops Nuance Free days at load & discharge must be agreed at booking. Demurrage (line’s container at port) and detention (line’s container outside port) escalate sharply once exhausted — often doubling after day 5. If supplier payment slips and release stalls, flag the daily $/container burn to Gurpreet immediately. Published rates are on the line’s website per port.
10

Booking Confirmation

Confirm the agreed shipment date with the carrier. If the date is missed for any reason, flag it immediately — do not wait.

Trade Ops Nuance A missed sailing rolls the cargo to the next vessel (often +7–14 days). That delay cascades into hedge timing (step 15), buyer delivery window, and Olea funding. One missed date can move three downstream dates — raise it the moment you know.
11

Purchase Invoice Booked

Receive and enter the supplier invoice into Phlo on the same day it arrives.

Trade Ops Nuance Three-way match: PO ↔ Invoice ↔ BL/Packing List. Quantities and per-bag prices must reconcile to the kg. Discrepancies become unresolved AP — and unresolved AP delays the BL endorsement that releases containers.
12

Documentation & Financing

Upload Bill of Lading, Packing List, and Purchase Contract to Phlo to give the finance team system access for processing.

Trade Ops Nuance On telex release BLs you don’t need to physically endorse; on originals, the courier chain and endorsement order matter. Finance triggers trade finance drawdowns off these docs — clean uploads = clean drawdowns.
13

Freight Payment

Process the freight invoice on the same day received. The shipping line will not release containers until freight + demurrage + detention + THC are fully cleared.

Trade Ops Nuance Reconcile the line’s invoice against the contract rate — surcharges (BAF, CAF, GRI, peak season) are where leakage occurs. Push back politely; lines correct routinely when challenged with the rate sheet.
03

Sales Track — From Buyer Contract to Cash in the Bank

Hedge · Release · Collect
14

Sales Contract

Prepare the sales contract on Phlo, mirroring the terms agreed in the purchase contract.

Trade Ops Nuance Mirroring is back-to-back protection. Watch for terms that don’t mirror: payment days (we may pay supplier 30d but get paid 60d → financing gap), and Incoterm (FOB in, DAP out → we carry the freight). Each gap is a working-capital cost — price it in.
15

Hedging — Manage Price Risk

The trader hedges all unpriced contracts on an ongoing basis. The coffee arrival date is critical: work backwards from expected delivery using the chosen line’s transit duration so hedge tenors and roll points align with cash flow.

Trade Ops Nuance Long physical purchase → sell ICE futures (Arabica ‘KC’ on ICE US, Robusta ‘RM’ on ICE Europe) to lock the flat price. Differentials remain unhedged — that’s the trader’s book. Roll the futures forward as you approach First Notice Day; never let physical position outrun the active futures month. Mind basis risk: KC settles in NY cents/lb, your invoice may be EUR/MT — FX overlay required.
16

Warehouse Release

Generate cargo release from Phlo on the same day it is requested. Only after cargo is offset — never before.

Trade Ops Nuance “Offset” means physical and futures legs are squared and the differential is priced. Releasing un-offset cargo creates a naked short/long on the trading book. Phlo enforces this control — do not seek workarounds.
17

Sales Invoice & Olea Factoring

Generate the sales invoice from Phlo within 1 working day of warehouse release. Send Olea the full document pack: Contract, Invoice, Warehouse Receipt (inward), Goods Receipt Note (outward). Olea pays us upfront and collects from the buyer on the assigned payment date. This is why continuous credit limit monitoring matters.

Trade Ops Nuance Factoring is non-recourse only up to the insured credit limit. Volume above the limit is recourse — if the buyer defaults on the excess, Olea claws back from us. The morning report should always show: limit, used, available, and any contracts headed for over-utilisation.
18

Logistics — AGC, Haulage & Inventory

AGC handles UK container clearance and invoices any demurrage/detention — rates billed have not always matched published rates; monitor closely. Arrange haulage to the buyer on the same day warehouse release is confirmed. Daily call with AGC for workload visibility is strongly recommended. On the 1st of each month (or next working day), obtain Warehouse Inventory from all warehouses (UK & Europe), share with Accounts, and update Phlo. Do not miss this step.

Trade Ops Nuance Spot-check 3 AGC invoices each month: container # → line’s public tariff → AGC invoice. Differences flag either a rate update or a margin AGC has slipped in. Inventory reports also feed hedge sizing — an inventory error becomes a hedge error becomes a P&L surprise.
19

Proof of Delivery (POD)

Submit the POD within 1 day of delivery. Must include lot number, delivery date, and buyer’s signature. An incomplete POD delays payment.

Trade Ops Nuance POD is the single document that converts “invoice issued” into “receivable funded” at Olea. Treat POD chasing as a Day-0 task, not a Day-3 task. Photograph the signed slip at the haulier’s gate; don’t rely on emailed scans landing.
20

Invoice Discounting

Submit all funding documents to Olea / finance within 24 hours of POD receipt.

Trade Ops Nuance Every day of delay = funding cost (SOFR + margin) we eat on our own working capital. On a $500k invoice at 7%, that’s ~$95/day — small per ticket, large per year. Speed here is direct margin.
21

Payments — Cargo

Cargo payment is approved by Gurpreet in line with contract terms.

Trade Ops Nuance Payment runs are weekly. Build the supplier-pay schedule against contract due dates and trade-finance facility availability; never let a supplier-pay slip just because the run cycle missed it — that’s how container release stalls (see step 9).
04

Reporting — The Daily Heartbeat

Single Source of Truth
22

Daily MIS Reporting

Trade Ops updates the Management Information System daily. All operations are tracked within Phlo ERP — this is the single source of truth for the business.

Trade Ops Nuance The MIS should at minimum surface: open positions (long/short by origin), hedge ratio, exposure by buyer vs limit, contracts unassigned >5 days, demurrage burn, monthly inventory, and AR ageing. If a metric isn’t in MIS, it isn’t managed.
⚠

Critical Rules — Non-Negotiable

Read Before You Act
  • Never proceed without PSS approval. Phlo blocks invoice generation and warehouse release until it is granted.
  • Warehouse release and invoice dates must match in Phlo ERP.
  • Warehouse release can only be generated after cargo is offset — never before.
  • Flag any contract unassigned for more than 5 days to Gurpreet.
  • Every POD must contain lot number, delivery date, and buyer’s signature. Incomplete PODs directly delay payment.
☷ Hedging Strategy — The Trader’s View

Why we hedge price risk

Coffee is a soft commodity with daily price swings of 2–5%. From the moment we buy unpriced physical, every cent move on ICE is P&L. Hedging converts speculative price exposure into a managed basis book.

Arabica vs Robusta two markets

Arabica (‘KC’) trades on ICE US, 37,500 lbs/contract, in US cents/lb. Robusta (‘RM’) trades on ICE Europe, 10 MT/contract, in USD/MT. Each origin in our book hedges to one of these references.

Differential pricing the basis

Most Origin contracts price as ICE month ± differential. Differentials reflect quality, origin, certification (Rainforest Alliance, organic), and freight. Differentials are not hedged — the trader manages this exposure deliberately.

Working the arrival date timing the roll

Hedge tenor is anchored to physical arrival, not contract date. Work backwards: arrival = vessel ETA + clearance. Roll the futures month before First Notice Day to avoid delivery; align hedge unwind with the buyer’s fixation call.

Risks the hedge does not remove residuals

Basis risk (futures vs physical move differently), FX risk (KC in USD, buyer in EUR/GBP), quality risk (PSS rejection), counterparty risk (buyer default). These need separate controls.

⎘ Coffee Trade Glossary
FOB
Free On Board — seller delivers cargo onto vessel; buyer (us) pays freight from that point.
CIF / DAP
CIF: seller pays Cost, Insurance, Freight to destination port. DAP: Delivered At Place — seller pays through to named buyer location.
ICE KC / RM
Arabica & Robusta futures contracts on Intercontinental Exchange — the global price benchmarks we hedge against.
PSS
Pre-Shipment Sample — QA-cupped representative sample; gate to ship.
BL
Bill of Lading — carrier’s receipt of cargo and document of title.
SI
Shipping Instructions — the data set issued to the line to draft the BL.
THC
Terminal Handling Charges — port-side handling fees billed by the line.
Demurrage
Charge for line’s container held inside port beyond free days.
Detention
Charge for line’s container held outside port (at warehouse, etc.) beyond free days.
POD
Proof of Delivery — buyer-signed slip; converts invoice to fundable receivable.
Phlo
Our ERP — single source of truth for contracts, positions, docs, invoices, MIS.
Olea
Factoring agent — pays us upfront on invoices, collects from buyer on due date.
AGC
UK container clearance agent — clears, hauls, bills line charges through to us.
Offset
Physical ↔ futures legs squared and differential priced — pre-condition for release.
